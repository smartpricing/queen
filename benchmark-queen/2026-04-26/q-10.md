## Headline takeaways

- **Throughput identical to `q-1`/`bp-10`**: 40 500 msg/s vs 39 130 (q-1) vs 39 060 (bp-10). Spread is < 4 %, well within run-to-run noise. **10 queues is essentially free** at this load level.
- **Pop batch efficiency went up** (41 → **98.57**) because the consumer's 50 connections are rotating through 10 different queue endpoints — each individual queue gets only ~5 connections of pressure, so per-queue partitions stay deeper between pops, and each pop returns nearly-full batches.
- **2 498 consumer timeouts** (autocannon-side) — when the consumer's rotation lands on a queue that's just been drained by the previous pop on the same queue, the long-poll holds longer. No server errors (server-side latencies stayed under 200 ms p99).
- **Per-queue load is well-balanced** (6 % spread between most/least consumed), confirming autocannon's request rotation distributes traffic evenly.
- **Postgres flat**: 100 % cache hit, ~7.5 vCPU queen, no errors. The 10 queues add zero measurable overhead.

> Multi-queue at 10 queues with constant total load: **no measurable cost in throughput, latency, or DB resource usage**. This is a good sign for multi-tenancy.
