> **New error: `evict_expired_waiting_messages` 30 s timeout**. With 10 010 `partition_consumers` rows and 10× more lease/watermark activity, the eviction job's CTE also tips over the 30 s `statement_timeout`. This is the same root cause as the `StatsService` bug — affects multiple background services. **One-line fix in both stored procedures (`SET LOCAL statement_timeout = 0;`)**.

## Headline takeaways for `bp-10-cg10`

- **Producer pays a 54 % tax for 10 consumer groups** — push goes from 39 k to 18 k msg/s. The cost increases sub-linearly with cg count (1 cg → 5 cg = 31 % cost; 5 cg → 10 cg = additional 23 %).
- **Total deliveries scale to 9.8×** — basically perfect. 16.1 M pushed × 9.8 = 158 M completed deliveries. **Pending = 0**, every cg fully drained.
- **Queen CPU scales sub-linearly** with cg count: 1 cg = 7.4 vCPU, 5 cg = 14.6, 10 cg = 18.1. The libqueen sidecar amortizes very well across groups.
- **Per-cg performance is uniformly fair**: 360 ± 1 req/s across all 10 groups, p99 within 5 ms of the median. Whatever Queen's group dispatch is doing, it's not picking favorites.
- **DB pool active stayed at 2.65** despite 500 consumer conns — Vegas controller doing what it's designed for.
- **`messages_consumed` growth is the tax**: 743 MB after 15 min for 10 cgs. At sustained load this would be ~70 GB/day on `messages_consumed` alone (one row per delivery). For a long-running multi-tenant deployment, that's the table to watch and ttl.
- **3 separate background-service timeouts fired**: StatsService (×3), eviction (×3), and 2 push deadlocks. **All absorbed by the failover/retry mechanisms — 0 lost messages, pending=0**. The same statement-timeout fix that closes the StatsService bug should be applied to `evict_expired_waiting_messages`.
