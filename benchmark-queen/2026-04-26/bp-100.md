## Headline takeaways

- **Throughput ceiling for 1×50 client config: ~104 k msg/s.** That's the highest sustained rate observed across all benchmarks so far. With **fewer connections (50) than the high-load partition axis (500)** but with `batch=100`, this configuration delivers **3.5× more messages/s** than `hi-part-1`'s 29 487 msg/s.
- **Batch axis curve**: 5 796 → 39 060 → **104 400** msg/s for batches 1 / 10 / 100.
  - 1 → 10: **6.74× speedup** (efficiency gain dominant)
  - 10 → 100: **2.67× speedup** (gains diminishing but still substantial)
  - Diminishing returns suggest **batch=1000** might add another 1.5-2×, before HTTP body size or DB INSERT planner cost catches up.
- **First sign of Postgres pressure**:
  - Cache hit ratio dropped to **99.99 %** (was 100 % in every other run)
  - PG memory hit **31.5 GiB** during run (would not fit on a 32 GiB VM)
  - `messages` table = **37 GB** in 15 min (would be 1.5 TB/day at this rate sustained)
- `**StatsService` finally tipped over**: 3 `[error] StatsService run_full_reconciliation error: canceling statement due to statement timeout` logs. The 30 s `statement_timeout` threshold was hit — same bug we saw in the long-running 14h test, now reproduced in 15 min under heavier sustained load. Confirms my earlier diagnosis: this is a real bug worth fixing.
- **Pop matched push at 100 % batch efficiency** — partitions stayed deep enough that every consumer batch returned the full 100 messages.
- **Queen CPU = ~19 vCPU**, RSS = 72 MB. Still very compact.

This is the most data-dense regime tested. `**batch=10` is the sensible default for production**: best per-message efficiency without catastrophic DB pressure. `batch=100` is the right knob to turn for "I have a brief burst of messages and want absolute max throughput", but be aware it pushes Postgres into a regime where the StatsService bug shows up.
