## Headline takeaways

This is a **big delta** between versions. At the production-realistic baseline:

1. **Push throughput dropped 22 %** in 0.12.19 (38.9k → 30.2k msg/s steady-state). Producer p99 went up ~25 % (38 → 48 ms).
2. **Pop throughput dropped 55 %** (38.4k → 17.1k msg/s). This is the dominant effect. Consumer can no longer keep up with producer at the same client config.
3. **Pop batch efficiency collapsed**: 42.29 → **15.31 msg/req**. Each pop on 0.12.19 returns 15 messages out of the requested 100. The pop path is fundamentally less efficient — likely the v1/v2 pop procedures don't drain partitions as completely as v3 does.
4. **Consumer falls 45 % behind by end of run** — pending = 12.96 M out of 28.6 M total. On 0.14, pending was 1.5 %.
5. **Postgres is much heavier**: same workload uses 28.5 GiB RSS vs 19 GiB. `messages_consumed` is 3.6× larger (305 MB vs 84 MB), which partly explains the pop-side inefficiency — old version tracks consumed differently.
6. **`StatsService` max cycle hit 16.8 s** here, on a workload that didn't stress 0.14's StatsService (bp-10 max was a few seconds). The reconciliation was **already heavy in 0.12.19** before the optimizations in v3 procedures.
7. **No errors, no DLQ, no ack-fails** in either version — durability is unchanged.

This single test already justifies the 0.13 → 0.14 work. The v3 push/pop procedure rewrites and the libqueen sidecar refinements (Vegas tuning, batch policy defaults raised on 2026-04-22) are doing real work.
