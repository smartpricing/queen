## Headline takeaways

1. **Both versions saturate the batch=100 axis below their HTTP-RPS ceiling**, but 0.14 saturates at **~104 k msg/s** and 0.12 saturates at **~64 k msg/s** — a **63 % advantage for 0.14** at peak throughput.
2. **The `StatsService` statement-timeout bug only manifests in 0.14 under this load** — 0.12 finished its full-reconciliation in 16 s vs 0.14's 30 s. Counterintuitively, 0.14's higher throughput is what's exposing the bug (more activity ⇒ bigger CTEs ⇒ closer to timeout).
3. **0.12 has higher event-loop lag** (max 44 ms vs ~0 ms) — the C++ event loop in 0.12 is more contended. This shows up as worse producer p50 (62 ms vs 45 ms) and p99 (330 ms vs 131 ms).
4. **Pop batch efficiency is 100 % in both** — when partitions are deep, both versions drain perfectly.
5. **Postgres uses MORE memory in 0.12** despite less throughput: 37.81 GiB at end vs 31.5 GiB. Combined with v12-bp-10's 28.5 vs 19 GiB, this confirms **0.14's PG footprint is leaner per message** (probably better autovacuum keep-up due to v3 procedure changes that produce fewer dead tuples).
